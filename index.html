What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple calculation using a person's height and weight. The formula is BMI = kg/m² where kg is a person's weight in kilograms and m² is their height in meters squared.

Formula: BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height² (m²)

BMI Categories Explained

Underweight (BMI < 18.5)
May indicate malnutrition or other health problems. Consider consulting a healthcare provider.
Normal Weight (BMI 18.5 - 24.9)
Indicates a healthy weight range. Maintain your current lifestyle and healthy habits.
Overweight (BMI 25 - 29.9)
May increase risk of health problems. Consider lifestyle changes including diet and exercise.
Obese (BMI ≥ 30)
Significantly increases risk of serious health conditions. Consult a healthcare provider for guidance.

Limitations of BMI

While BMI is a useful screening tool, it has some limitations:

  • Does not distinguish between muscle and fat mass
  • May not accurately reflect body composition in athletes
  • Does not account for age, gender, or ethnicity differences
  • Should be used alongside other health assessments

Health Tips

For Maintaining Healthy Weight:

  • Eat a balanced diet with plenty of fruits and vegetables
  • Exercise regularly (at least 30 minutes daily)
  • Stay hydrated - drink 8 glasses of water per day
  • Get adequate sleep (7-8 hours per night)
  • Manage stress effectively
  • Regular health check-ups

When to Seek Medical Advice:

Consult a healthcare provider if:

  • Your BMI is outside the healthy range
  • You have concerns about your weight
  • You're planning significant dietary or exercise changes
  • You have other health risk factors

Disclaimer: BMI is a screening tool and not a diagnostic tool. Always consult with healthcare professionals for personalized medical advice.
